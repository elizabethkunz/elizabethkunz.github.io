About

I am an undergraduate at USC studying physics. I’m currently doing research in superconducting quantum hardware, specifically in simulating, designing and fabricating nanoscale superconducting devices. I am interested in further exploring the fields of quantum computing and information, condensed matter, and open quantum systems.

Research

I have been working for the Levenson-Falk Lab since May 2024. The LFL uses superconducting circuits to advance quantum computing and fundamental physics research. The LFL designs on-chip solutions to common problems in quantum computing with superconducting qubits.

I am a developer for the SQuADDS project, a validated design database and simulation workflow software for superconducting qubit design.

Previous to working on SQuADDS, I fabricate superconducting electronics for applications in quantum information science at the John O'Brien Nanofabrication Laboratory at USC.
